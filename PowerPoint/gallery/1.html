Greg Betza is an illustrator, artist and designer from New Jersey. He does not limit his clients with a predetermined style, but with communication and aesthetics as his focus. He works in many mediums and styles in order to produce a solution as unique as the problem it is solving. His versatility in style and unique approach has led him to create work for a diverse group of clients, among them are E&J Gallo Winery, DDB, and The Wall Street Journal. Greg has received numerous awards from institutions such as the Society of Illustrators of LA, Communication Arts, and American Illustration.

Dale Edwin Murray is a freelance illustrator and graphic artist living and working in London. Inspired by 50s cartoons, screenprinting and vintage posters, he works across editorial illustrations, art prints and t-shirt graphics for a wide range of clients including Toyota, BBC and Virgin.

Kendra Binney paints imaginary worlds of dripping landscapes and insecure vulnerable characters. Draped in memories, remorse, and fragile realities. They are at once gentle and cruel, sweet and unsettling. She currently lives in Portland, OR where she spends her days all alone in a small studio. There she paints, daydreams, and paints some more.

Twenty Seven year old photographer Kyle Johnson loves shooting people, no matter if its portraiture, fashion, or working with a band which he does quite often in his current town of Seattle, WA. Kyle strives to create images that are classically executed often with a hint of humor. His photos have been featured in VICE, DAZED & Confused, Wallpaper*, and most recently Self-Titled Magazine.

Mia Nolting grew up in California and moved to Portland to attend the Pacific Northwest College of Art, where she earned her BFA in 2008. In addition to having shown work internationally, she works on illustration and design projects for clients such as Gnu Snowboards, The New York Times, and Penguin Books. Influences include: the human body, typography, romance, Saul Steinberg, wildflowers, mornings, telepathy, handwriting, communication, small-press books, and young-adulthood.

Rose Stallard is a real rock 'n' roller, and this comes through in everything she does. She mixes up her own drawings with found images from across the board, injecting fanzine-like enthusiasm (and discernment). She screen prints onto canvas, t-shirts or paper, then re-works the images with materials like letraset, spray paint, felt-tip, stitching and paint. More than just a homage though - Rose is really exploring her own loving relationship with illustration and rock as an artist, musician and fan.

Rose's clients include Sunday Times Style, The Guardian, Simpson London, Urban Outfitters, Dazed and Confused, Oxfam, Fcuk, Topman and Topshop.

In addition to all of the above Rose is one of the founders and creative director of Print Club London.

You might also catch her playing with her band Ten Miles of Bad Road.
